Learning Objectives

By the end of this lab, you will be able to:

  • Explain the logic of null hypothesis significance testing
  • Choose the appropriate t-test for different research designs
  • Conduct a one-sample t-test using t.test()
  • Conduct an independent samples t-test comparing two groups
  • Conduct a paired samples t-test for pre-post designs
  • Calculate and interpret Cohen’s d effect sizes
  • Check assumptions for t-tests
  • Write APA-style results for t-tests

The Data Story

We’ve collected data from a Test Anxiety intervention study:

  • 80 university students completed the Test Anxiety Inventory
  • Participants were assigned to either Control or Intervention groups
  • Anxiety was measured before and after the intervention
  • The population mean for this scale is known to be 50

Our research questions: 1. Does our sample differ from the population norm? 2. Do the groups differ at baseline? 3. Did anxiety change from pre to post?

Note

Learn More About Test Anxiety

Test anxiety affects academic performance and wellbeing. Understanding how to measure and reduce it is crucial for educational psychology.

## The Data Story

We've collected data from a Test Anxiety intervention study:

- **80 university students** completed the Test Anxiety Inventory
- Participants were assigned to either **Control** or **Intervention** groups
- Anxiety was measured **before and after** the intervention
- The population mean for this scale is known to be **50**

**Our research questions**:
1. Does our sample differ from the population norm?
2. Do the groups differ at baseline?
3. Did anxiety change from pre to post?
